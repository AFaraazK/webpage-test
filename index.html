The Basics of Building a Good Setting

Only plan for the essentials, you can improvise the rest

As the morning sun crests over the jagged mountaintops, your party enters the tavern, looking for... wait what was the mayor called again? The tavern name? I'm not sure, give me a second...

As a writer, and especially as a DM you have to go through a number of different settings, preparing each one just enough that the players are able to follow their quest, without waiting as you hurriedly trying to come up with names and descriptions. But what do you need to focus on? How do you build a setting that feels complete without spending a Tolkein-esque amount sat around a map?

The Checklist:

  • Purpose
  • Location
  • Leadership
  • Vibes

Questions you need to ask yourself:

  1. What's the point?
    • Why are the players coming to this town?
    • Are they looking for something? Someone?
    • Is this a simple rest stop between questions?
  2. Where is it located?
    • How does that effect the climate, makeup or feel of the town?
